Texas Coach Indicted on Federal Charges of Sexual Exploitation of Children 
WFAA (ABC-8) (11/14, Snyder) reported that William Caleb Elliott, a former Celina ISD teacher and coach, has been 
indicted on eight federal charges of sexual exploitation of children. According to the article, the charges stem from 
allegations that Elliott took videos and photos of student athletes in a locker room in various states of undress or 
nude using his cellphone. A Department of Justice press release noted that this case is being investigated by the 
Celina Police Department, Collin County District Attorney's Office, and the FBI. Additional reporting was provided 
by Star Local Media (11/15, Albanese). 
Pennsylvania Man Sentenced for Luring Girl for Sex, Possessing Images of Minors 
Penn Live (11/14, Prose) reported that Ryan Peters, a 36-year-old man from McKees Rocks, Pennsylvania, has been 
sentenced to 14 years in federal prison. According to the article, he was convicted of attempted coercion and 
enticement of a minor to engage in illegal sexual activity and possession of child sexual abuse material. The article 
noted that Peters had tried to meet an FBI agent posing as a 12-year-old girl for sex and was found to have explicit 
images and videos of minors. Additional reporting was provided by WPXI (NBC-11) (11/14, Staff Writer). 
Convicted Sex Offender Receives Additional Sentence for Threatening Victim's Mother 
Hoodline Washington DC (11/15, Choudhury) reported that Franklin Jovany Torres, a 42-year-old convicted sex 
offender, received an additional 16-month sentence for sending threatening letters to the mother of his underage 
victim. According to the article, Torres pleaded guilty to mailing menacing communications and will serve the new 
sentence in addition to his current 21-year term. The article noted that the FBI investigated the case after a mother 
and son reported sexual abuse when they found a photo on Torres' Facebook page; "The boy victim's parents had 
allowed Torres to stay in their apartment after Torres lost his job and needed a place to stay," as stated by the U.S. 
Attorney's Office. The threats were made while Torres was in prison, and he had previously been convicted of 
producing, distributing, and possessing child pornography, as well as first degree sexual abuse of a minor 
$50K Reward Offered for Info on Pennsylvania Kidnapping Suspect 
Patch (11/14, Bennett) reported that a $50,000 reward is being offered for information leading to the apprehension 
of Trung Duc Lu, a suspect in a 2014 Philadelphia kidnapping and murder case. According to the article, Lu is 
wanted on numerous charges, including conspiracy to commit kidnapping, extortion, and racketeering, in 
connection with the torture and murder of two Vietnamese brothers. The article noted that the FBI is seeking tips 
to locate Lu, who has ties to New York City and was last confirmed to be in the Republic of Vietnam. Additional 
reporting was provided by WPG-FM (11/15, Coleman). 
Suspected Child Predators Arrested in Florida 
WPBF (ABC-25) (11/14, Rozier) reported that six individuals were arrested in Martin County, Florida, for allegedly 
trying to meet with who they thought were 14-year-olds as part of a joint operation called "All Tricks, No Treats". 
According to the article, the operation, which involved undercover officers tracking down alleged child predators 
online, resulted in 18 individuals interacting with detectives, with half of them sending sexually explicit 
photos. FDLE, Stuart Police, FBI, and Homeland Security Investigations were among the agencies at Thursday's 
news conference at the Martin County Sheriff's Office. The article noted that the six individuals in custody could 
face a minimum of 42 months behind bars, according to Tom Bakkedahl, state attorney for the 19th Judicial Circuit. 
Child Predators Busted in Georgia 
Hoodline Atlanta (11/14, Mehta) reported that a coalition of law enforcement agencies, including the FBI, arrested 
four individuals (Jhirmichael Williams, Bryon Willis, Samantha Reese, and Samantha Bradford) on charges of child 
exploitation. According to the article, the accused face charges including sex trafficking of a minor, production of 
child pornography, and enticement of a minor. FBI Special Agent in Charge of Atlanta, Paul Brown, stressed the 
criticality of protecting children and highlighted the collaborative effort to hold perpetrators accountable. The 
article noted that the investigation is ongoing, and authorities are encouraging anyone with information to contact 
the FBI.
