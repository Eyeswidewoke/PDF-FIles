Crypto News (11/20, Shittu) reported that William Lonergan Hill, Samourai Wallet's CTO, was sentenced to 4 years 
in federal prison for his role in operating a Bitcoin mixing platform that processed over $237 million in known 
criminal proceeds. According to the article, Hill and Samourai CEO Keonne Rodriguez pleaded guilty to conspiracy 
to operate an unlicensed money-transmitting business, with Rodriguez receiving a 5-year prison sentence. A 
Department of Justice press release noted that US Attorney Nicholas Roos praised the investigative work of the 
Internal Revenue Service-Criminal Investigation and the FBI. He also acknowledged the assistance of the Justice 
Department's Office of International Affairs, Europol, the Portuguese Judicial Police, the Procuradoria-Geral da 
RepUblica, the Icelandic Police, and the FBI Field Office in Pittsburgh for their assistance in the investigation of this 
case. 
Twelve Charged in $11 Million Stolen Check Scheme Targeting Pandemic Relief Funds 
RLS Media (11/20, Smith) reported that twelve individuals, including Wayne Bessant, Britany Brown, and others, 
have been charged in a bank fraud conspiracy targeting COVID-19 business relief programs. According to the 
article, the scheme, which ran from March 2023 to May 2025, involved stealing over 100 checks, including US 
Treasury checks, totaling over $11 million. "Just as the twelve subjects schemed and conspired to cheat the system 
by depositing stolen checks meant for COVID-19 pandemic relief efforts, the FBI and our federal partners worked 
together to follow the facts and evidence that would lead to this charge. This case is a textbook example of the 
partnerships we build to prevent fraud against the Government and protect taxpayers from fraud in any form," said 
FBI Newark Special Agent in Charge Stefanie Roddy in a Department of Justice press release. 
Court Ultimatum for Cryptoqueen Wanted by FBI 
BBC News (11/20, Byrne) reported that Ruja Ignatova, known as the "Cryptoqueen", has been given 28 days to 
object to her assets being seized by Guernsey's government. According to the article, the order is being sought on 
behalf of German authorities to recover funds from the sale of two London properties once owned by Ignatova, to 
compensate OneCoin victims who lost over $4bn. The article noted that Ignatova, who has been missing for 8 years 
and is on the FBI's Ten Most Wanted list, has until December 16 to respond to the forfeiture order. The FBI believes 
Ms Ignatova travels with armed guards and may have had plastic surgery to alter her appearance. 
Kansas Man Sentenced to Federal Prison for Trafficking Firearms Into Connecticut 
JC Post (11/20, Staff Writer) reported that Brian Baker, a 48-year-old Kansas man, was sentenced to 60 months in 
prison for his role in a nationwide gun trafficking ring. According to the article, Baker, a convicted felon, used straw 
purchasers to buy firearms in Kansas and shipped them to Connecticut and other locations. The article noted that, 
according to court documents and statements made in court, in May 2023, members of the FBI Waterbury Safe 
Streets Task Force conducted three controlled purchases of a total of nine firearms and one controlled purchase of 
cocaine from Luis Perez in Waterbury. 
Maine Man Sentenced for Making Interstate Threats 
WGME (CBS-13) (11/20, St. Pierre) reported that Benjamin Brown, a 47-year-old Waterville man, has been 
sentenced to time served and three years of supervised release for making violent online threats. According to the 
article, Brown pleaded guilty to making threatening interstate communications, which included threats to hunt 
down and kill immigrants, as well as threats against politicians such as Sen. Susan Collins and Joe Biden. The 
threats were made in online posts, including on YouTube, where Brown also challenged the FBI and claimed he 
would participate in a revolution to overthrow the government. According to an affidavit, Brown also challenged 
the FBI directly: "Hey FBI, sorry to be the bearer of bad news, but there is nothing that you'll be able to do to stop 
the revolution because we are too many and we are too powerful. We will overthrow you, we will overthrow the 
CIA, we will overthrow every police officer in this country who chooses to fight on your side, we will overthrow 
every single military member who chooses to fight on your side. We outnumber you, we outgun you, and you will 
not win." 
Woman Accused of Assaulting Federal Officer at North Carolina ICE Facility 
WCNC (NBC-36) (11/20, Puente) reported that a 44-year-old woman, Heather Morrow, has been charged with 
assaulting a federal officer following a confrontation outside a U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement 
Enforcement and Removal Operations facility in Charlotte. According to federal prosecutors, Morrow was among a 
group gathered on November 16 in an apparent attempt to block law enforcement activity on the property. When 
a federal officer arrived for work, several individuals allegedly obstructed the entrance, and one person struck the 
officer's vehicle. As the officer moved to arrest that individual, prosecutors say Morrow approached from behind, 
grabbed the officer's shoulders, and attempted to jump onto the officer's back, constituting forcible interference.
