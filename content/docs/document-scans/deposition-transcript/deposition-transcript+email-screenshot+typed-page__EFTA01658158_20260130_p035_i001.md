Trump Says Pelosi, Biden Opposed His Decision To Ban Travelers From China. 
In a tweet Sunday morning, President Trump said House Speaker Pelosi opposed his decision to 
ban people from China from entering US even though it saved "tens of thousands of lives." 
Trump added that Joe Biden agreed with Pelosi, but later admitted Trump was correct. Trump 
wrote, "Crazy Nancy Pelosi said I made a mistake when I banned people from infected China 
from entering the U.S. in January. Tens of thousands of lives were saved, as she danced in the 
Streets of Chinatown (SF) in late February. Biden agreed with her, but soon admitted that I was 
right!" 
AP-NORC Poll Shows Trump's Approval On Virus Falling. 
The AP (7/26, Pace, Fingerhut) reports a new AP-NORC Center for Public Affairs Research poll 
found that with the election 100 days away, "more Americans say the country is heading in the 
wrong direction than at any previous point in Donald Trump's presidency, putting the incumbent 
in a perilous position as his reelection bid against Democrat Joe Biden enters a pivotal stretch." 
The poll shows "Trump's approval for his handling of the COVID-19 pandemic falling to a new 
low, with just 32% of Americans supportive of his approach." Moreover, "8 in 10 Americans say 
the country is heading in the wrong direction," and "just 38% of Americans say the national 
economy is good, down from 67% in January, before the pandemic upended most aspects of 
everyday life."
