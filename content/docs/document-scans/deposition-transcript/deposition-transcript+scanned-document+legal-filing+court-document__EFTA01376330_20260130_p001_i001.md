Any Collateral Obligation other than a Defaulted Obligation with a Moody's

Obligation. 
"Caa/CCC Excess": The greater of the Caa Excess Amount and the CCC Excess Amount.

aggregate Market Value of Caa Collateral Obligations, or, in the case of Caa Obligations that arc Discount 
Obligations, the lesser of their purchase price and Market Value (in order of ascending Mattel Value or purchase

Excess Amount, the aggregate Market Value of the CCC Collateral Obligations (in order of ascending Market 
Value, starting with the CCC Collateral Obligation with the lowest Market Value) with an aggregate principal 
balance equal to the CCC Excess Amount. 
"Calculation Agent": The Bank or. for so long as any of the Notes remain Outstanding. such other agent. reasonably 
acceptable to the Investment Manager, as may be appointed to calculate LIBOR in respect of each Interest Period in 
accordance with the terms of the Indenture. 
"CCC Collateral Obligation-: Any Collateral Obligation other than a Defaulted Obligation with an S&P Rating of 
"CCC+" or lower. 
"CCC Excess Amount": The aggregate principal balance of CCC Collateral Obligations in excess of 7.5% of the 
Portfolio Principal Balance. 
"Central Bank": The Central Bank of Ireland. 
"Class": All of (a) the Notes having the same Interest Rate. Stated Maturity and designation and (b) the Preferred

will vote together as a single class except as specified, and (y) any Subordinated Securities entitled to vote on a 
matter will vote as a single class.

calculated for the Class A Notes as the Applicable Notes is at least 124.7%.
