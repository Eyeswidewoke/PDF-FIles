MARCO MAGNANI 
Marco Magnani, a native of Parma Italy and a citizen of both Italy and the U.S.A., 
currently resides in Milan with his wife Laurence. 
Mr. Magnani is currently a Managing Director at Mediobanca S.p.A. who is 
responsible for Merger & Acquisition and Corporate Finance transactions primarily 
in Italy, Continental Europe and the United States. 
Prior to this, for almost a decade Mr. Magnani lived in New York, where he worked 
in Investment Banking on Wall Street, first at Credit Suisse First Boston and then 
at J.P. Morgan's global headquarters. During this period he provided financial 
advice to several U.S. and international clients regarding mergers, acquisitions 
and other corporate finance transactions in the U.S., Europe and Asia, attaining 
the position of Vice President at the age of 29 and subsequently becoming Senior 
Vice President in the M&A Group. 
Mr. Magnani holds both a U.S. and an Italian high school degree received with top 
marks. His undergraduate degree is in Economics from the University of Rome 
"La Sapienza", where he graduated with 110/110 summa cum laude, and his 
Master in Business Administration degree from Columbia University in New York 
where he obtained a double-major in Finance and Management of Organizations, 
as a result of which he obtained a summer position at Goldman Sachs in London. 
Mr. Magnani was awarded an A.F.S. (American Field Service) scholarship at 16 
years of age to spend a year in the United States attending a local high school and 
living in an American family. He was also awarded a merit-based full four year 
scholarship by the Federazione Nazionale Cavalieri del Lavoro to attend its College 
in Rome, successfully meeting all its requirements'. After graduation, he won the 
prestigious "Luciano Jona" scholarship for post-graduate economic and financial 
studies, which completely covered the costs of his program at Columbia 
University. He also completed a Global Leadership and Public Policy for the 21st 
Century program at Harvard University, John F. Kennedy School of Government. 
In 2010 he has been honored by the World Economic Forum as a Young Global 
Leader'. He is an active member of The Forum of YGLs, a "dynamic, diverse global 
community of the world's most outstanding, next generation leaders nominated 
under the age of 40 who commit a portion of their time to jointly shape a better 
future and thereby improve the state of the world".
