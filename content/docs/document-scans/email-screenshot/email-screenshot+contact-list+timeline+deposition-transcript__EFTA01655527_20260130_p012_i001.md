Florida Teen Gets Three Years in Federal Prison for Machine Gun Possession 
Tampa Free Press (11/14, Shultz) reported that Sean Rayvon Hubbard, Jr., a 19-year-old from Ocala, has been 
sentenced to three years in federal prison for possession of a machine gun. According to the article, Hubbard 
pleaded guilty to the charge on May 22, 2025, after a joint investigation by the FBI and the Ocala Police 
Department. The article noted that the case involved a handgun that was illegally modified to fire multiple rounds 
with a single trigger pull, with evidence linking Hubbard to the weapon found on his cell phone. 
Utah Man Accused of Damaging ICE Van Pleads Guilty 
KTVX (ABC-4) (11/15, Jones, Trauntvein) reported that Ryan Michael Gaines, 32, pleaded guilty to damaging 
government property exceeding $1,000, a felony. According to the article, the damage to an ICE van in St. George, 
Utah, totaled $2,883 and occurred in April. The article noted that the case was investigated jointly by St. George 
Police and the FBI Salt Lake City Field Office, St. George Resident Agency. Additional reporting was provided by 
Hoodline Salt Lake City (11/15, Warner). 
Six Massachusetts Businesses Busted in Inspections 
Watertown News (11/14, Breitrose) reported that six body works businesses in Watertown were closed indefinitely 
due to violations of city and state regulations. According to the article, the inspections were conducted by the 
Watertown Health Department, Watertown Police, and other agencies, including the FBI Human Trafficking Task 
Force. The article noted that the businesses will not be allowed to reopen until they meet the required standards 
set by the Watertown Board of Health, Building Department, Zoning Department, and the Massachusetts Division 
of Occupational Licensure. Additional reporting was provided by WBZ (CBS-4) (11/14, Mutasa), WCVB (ABC-5) 
(11/14, Haynes), and WFXT (FOX-25) (11/14, Karedes). 
Illinois Man Accused of Making Threats Against Michigan Mosque Arrested 
The Detroit News (11/14, Aguilar) reported that Kenneth Gehrke, a 63-year-old Illinois man, was arrested for 
allegedly making threats against a Dearborn Heights mosque in September. According to the article, Gehrke was 
booked into the Wayne County jail on a felony charge of false report or threat of terrorism and is currently in 
custody. The article noted that the arrest was a result of a collaborative effort between law enforcement agencies, 
including the Dearborn Heights Police Department, the FBI, and the Wayne County Sheriff's Office. 
Oregon Man Facing Child Sex Abuse Charges Following Investigation 
KEZI (ABC-9) (11/14, Bonham) reported that a 27-year-old Eugene man, James Michael Minton, was arrested after 
a search warrant was served at a west Eugene apartment. According to the article, Minton faces charges including 
three counts of first-degree and three counts of second-degree encouraging child sex abuse. The article noted 
that the search warrant was part of the Lane County Internet Crimes Against Children Task Force's ongoing 
investigation into child exploitation. The Lane County Sheriffs Office received support from the Oregon 
Department of Justice and the FBI. 
Alaska Man Sentenced for Exploiting Three Minors 
Fairbanks Daily News-Miner (11/16, Staff Writer) reported that Matthew Jackson, a 23-year-old from Shaktoolik, 
was sentenced to 30 years in prison for sexually exploiting three minors and cyberstalking them and an adult. 
According to the article, Jackson pleaded guilty to the charges in May and used tactics such as coercion and 
supplying marijuana and alcohol to exploit his victims. The article noted that Anchorage Field Office FBI Acting 
Special Agent in Charge Brandon Waddle said Jackson's sentence should serve as a "message to anyone who 
commits crimes against children in Alaska." "The FBI and our partners will relentlessly pursue and hold predators 
accountable to the fullest extent of the law," Waddle said.
