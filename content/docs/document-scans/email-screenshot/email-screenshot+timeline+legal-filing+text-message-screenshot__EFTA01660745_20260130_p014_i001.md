WTXF (FOX-29) (07/02, Staff Writer) reported that Brian Healion, a 33-year-old member of the 
Philadelphia chapter of the Proud Boys, was sentenced to 100 days in prison and 36 months of supervised 
release for his involvement in the January 6 Capitol riot. According to the article, Healion, who was part 
of a subgroup within the Proud Boys known as the Ministry of Self-Defense, participated in the breach, 
leading chants and crossing police barriers during the event. The article noted that he was arrested by the 
FBI on December 10, 2021, in Upper Darby, and the ongoing investigation has led to over 1,450 people 
being charged nationwide, including more than 500 for assaulting or impeding law enforcement. 
Judge Grants Search Warrant for Conservative Influencer's Phone in Jan. 6 
Investigation 
WUSA (CBS-9) (07/02, Fischer) reported that Chief Judge James Boasberg of the D.C. District Court 
authorized a search warrant for the phone of conservative influencer Isabella Maria DeLuca, reversing a 
prior decision that deemed the potential evidence as too outdated. According to the article, this decision 
came after the Justice Department demonstrated probable cause, arguing that digital data could still be 
retrieved from DeLuca's devices via her iCloud account despite the time elapsed since the January 6 
Capitol riot. The article noted that DeLuca, who participated in the riot and allegedly facilitated the theft 
and use of a table as a weapon against police, faces multiple misdemeanor charges and was arrested in 
California in March. 
Virginia School Board Member Sentenced for Participation in Jan. 6 Capitol Riot 
WJLA (ABC-7) (07/02, Lockman) reported that Miles Brandon Adkins, a school board member from 
Frederick County, Virginia, was sentenced to two years of probation, including 12 days of intermittent 
confinement, for his involvement in the January 6 Capitol riot. According to the article, the FBI obtained 
Facebook messages sent by Adkins detailing his plans for the trip to D.C. and his activities on the day, 
which included entering the Capitol after attending former President Trump's rally. The article noted that 
Adkins also mentioned previous trips to D.C. to escort individuals associated with the Oath Keepers, and 
as part of his sentence, he was ordered to pay $500 in restitution and a $1,000 fine. 
Arkansas Man Pleads Guilty to Charges After Entering U.S. Capitol During Jan. 6 
Riot 
KTHV (CBS-11) (07/02, Banks) reported that Daniel Hatcher, a 73-year-old from Little Rock, pleaded guilty 
to multiple charges including disorderly conduct and parading in a Capitol building, related to his actions 
during the January 6 riot. According to the article, Hatcher was arrested on February 12 after the FBI 
received tips and conducted interviews with him on January 13 and 15, 2021, where he admitted to 
attending Trump's rally and entering the Capitol. The article noted that the Department of Justice 
outlined that Hatcher could face up to six months in prison, a probationary period of up to five years, and 
a fine of up to $5,000. 
Attorneys Face Deadline to Wrap Jan. 6 Prosecutions. That Could Slide if Trump 
Wins 
CBS News (07/02, MacFarlane) reported that the Supreme Court decision affecting Jan. 6 defendants has 
introduced uncertainty regarding the future of obstruction charges, possibly influencing cases against 
nearly 250 individuals charged with such offenses during the Capitol riot. According to the article, the FBI 
is actively seeking help to identify individuals from video footage who were involved in violent assaults on 
federal officers during the incident. The article highlighted the extensive scope of the investigations, 
noting that with the statute of limitations extending to January 2026, federal prosecutors are under 
pressure to wrap up cases. They may be further complicated by potential pardons Donald Trump has 
suggested he might grant if reelected.
