KFGO-FM (11/14, Hjelmstad) reported that Jordain Smith, a 26-year-old Warwick, North Dakota man, was 
sentenced to 20 years in prison for sexually abusing girls. According to the article, Smith pled guilty to two charges 
involving the sexual abuse of two minors and will serve 25 years of supervised release after his prison 
sentence. "Today's sentence is one more step forward in the pursuit of justice for the two minor females who were 
viciously abused by Jordain laden Smith," said FBI Minneapolis Special Agent in Charge Alvin M. Winston, Sr. "Those 
who sexually exploit children will meet the full force of the law. The FBI has no higher calling than to protect 
innocent children from sexual predators like Smith. We are proud of our work with our Federal, State, and Local 
partners on this investigation." The article noted that the case was investigated by the FBI and prosecuted as part 
of Project Safe Childhood, an initiative to protect children from online exploitation and abuse. 
Two Dozen Charged With Child Sex Crimes After FBI's Operation Summer Heat 
KSHB (NBC-41) (11/14, Staff Writer) reported that the FBI's Summer Heat Initiative resulted in 24 people being 
charged with child sex crimes. According to the article, 15 of the 24 people were arrested in August, with charges 
including electronic solicitation, coercion and enticement of a minor, and distribution of child 
pornography. "Today's announcement reiterates law enforcement's commitment to protecting our children," said 
Stephen Cyrus, FBI Special Agent in Charge of the Kansas City Field Office, in a news release. "We must continue to 
protect our most vulnerable from harm." The article noted that the operation involved law officers from Missouri, 
Kansas, and other states. Additional reporting was provided by KZRG-FM (11/14, Smith) and KMBC (ABC-9) (11/14, 
Davis). 
Man Arrested After Allegedly Robbing North Texas Bank 
KTVT (CBS-11) (11/14, Brown) reported that a 25-year-old man from Waco allegedly robbed a Bank of America in 
Bedford. According to the article, the suspect fled the scene on a bicycle after demanding money from a teller. The 
article noted that the man was arrested with the assistance of the FBI and Bedford Police and will be transferred to 
the Tarrant County Jail. Additional reporting was provided by WFAA (ABC-8) (11/15, Snyder). 
Louisiana Man Sentenced to Federal Prison for Texas and Louisiana Bank Robberies 
My Texas Daily (11/14, Richards) reported that Charleston Jamal Washington, a 39-year-old man from Lafayette, 
Louisiana, has been sentenced to 168 months in federal prison. According to the article, he pleaded guilty to 
committing armed bank robberies in Texas and Louisiana, including robberies at Home Bank in Welsh, Louisiana, 
and First State Bank in Vidor, Texas. The article noted that the investigation involved multiple agencies, including 
the FBI's Beaumont Resident Agency, the FBI's Lake Charles Resident Agency, and the Lafayette Resident Agency, as 
well as several local police departments. 
FBI Seeks Assistance in Identifying Suspect in Illinois Bank Robbery 
Fox Valley Magazine (11/14, Stukel) reported that the FBI is seeking assistance in identifying a suspect involved in 
an armed bank robbery at US Bank in St. Charles on November 8. According to the article, the suspect is described 
as a masked man wearing a camouflage hoodie and a Chicago Bears baseball cap; photos from surveillance 
cameras have also been released. The article noted that the FBI is encouraging the public to reach out if they have 
any information pertaining to the case. Additional information was provided by Shaw Local (11/14, Weslo) and 
Patch (11/14, Schanezer).

Russian Alleged Cyber-Hacker Faces Extradition to US After Arrest in Thailand 
CNN (11/15, Sharman, Regan, Lyngaas) reported that Denis Obrezko, a 35-year-old Russian man, has been arrested, 
in a joint operation between the FBI and Thai authorities, for alleged cyber-crime and faces extradition to the US. 
According to the article, Obrezko is allegedly part of the Void Blizzard cyber espionage gang, which is known for 
hacking attacks that align with Kremlin interests. The article noted that he will be held in Bangkok pending 
extradition to the US, where he is wanted for breaching security systems and attacking government agencies in 
Europe and the US.
