WPDE (ABC-15) (09/17, Brown) reported that a federal judge has approved a speedy trial for Jason Barrett, charged 
in connection with the January 6, 2021, Capitol insurrection, following his arrest by FBI agents two weeks ago. 
According to the article, the charges included assaulting police officers and obstructing law enforcement, with an 
affidavit detailing Barrett's participation in the riot, including grabbing an officer from behind and throwing a police 
helmet. The article detailed that the FBI identified Barrett through social media, and his involvement is highlighted 
by footage showing him at the front of the mob as they breached the Capitol grounds. 
Oregonian Pleads Guilty on Jan. 6 Charges, Washington Man Is Sentenced 
Oregon Public Broadcast (OPB) (9/17, Staff) reported that two men from the Pacific Northwest, Andy Steven Oliva-
Lopez and Benjamin John Silva, are facing legal consequences for their roles in the January 6, 2021, Capitol attack. 
The article specified that FBI investigations led to Oliva-Lopez's guilty plea for assaulting officers by spraying them 
with bear spray during the riots, while Silva was sentenced for obstructing law enforcement, participating in a push 
against police at the Capitol. The U.S. Attorney's Office noted that over 1,500 individuals have been charged in 
connection with the Capitol breach, which interrupted the electoral vote count. KIRO (CBS-7) (09/17, Staff) added 
that Silva will serve 24 months of supervised release, including four months of home confinement, and was 
ordered to pay $2,000 in restitution in addition to the prison sentence. KGW (NBC-8) (09/17, Xiaoshi DePaola) 
and KPTV (FOX-12) (09/17, Staff) also reported on the story. 
Two Men Arrested in Northwest Ohio for Connections to Jan. 6 Insurrection 
WTVG (ABC-13) (09/17, Staff) reported that two men from northwest Ohio, Todd Michael Bills and Richard 
Gutowski, have been arrested for their alleged involvement in the January 6, 2021, Capitol riots. According to the 
article, the FBI utilized cellphone tracking and Google Maps to confirm Bills' presence at the Capitol, where he 
reportedly climbed scaffolding and breached police lines; he faces four federal charges, with his case being 
transferred to D.C. federal court. The article noted that Gutowski self-reported to the FBI about his actions during 
the riot, admitting to trespassing inside the Capitol, and he is currently facing five federal charges. 
DOJ Wants Four Years in Prison for Pastor Who Used Bullhorn to Incite Mob on Jan. 6 
WUSA (CBS-9) (09/17, Fischer) reported that William Dunfee, an Ohio pastor identified as a "ground commander" 
during the January 6, 2021, Capitol riot, faces a recommendation of four years in prison for his actions. The article 
explained that the FBI linked Dunfee to the riot through his company-branded jacket, noting that he led chants, 
urged rioters to breach police barricades, and was involved in organizing attacks on law enforcement. According to 
the article, during the chaos, he was quoted saying, "We want Donald Trump... Give us Trump or give us our 
house!" and celebrated the disruption of the electoral certification by exclaiming, "Hallelujah! Mission 
accomplished!" 
Montana Man Convicted in Jan. 6 Insurrection Speaks to '60 Minutes' 
Ravalli Republic (09/17, Drake) reported that Jerod Hughes, an East Helena man convicted for his role in the 
January 6, 2021, Capitol insurrection, expressed his belief in a stolen election during a recent "60 Minutes" 
interview, framing his actions as patriotic. The article added that he and his brother pleaded guilty to obstruction of 
an official proceeding, with Hughes acknowledging his involvement by saying, "I share some of the responsibility of 
what happened that day," and admitting he shouldn't have yelled at police officers. According to the article, U.S. 
Attorney Matthew Graves noted that the prosecution focused on individuals who entered the Capitol and engaged 
in violent behavior, emphasizing that 1,000 defendants have been convicted, with ongoing FBI efforts to identify 
additional suspects. 
Continued Reporting: West Virginia Man Indicted for Actions During January 6 Capitol Breach 
WOWK (CBS-13) (09/17, Meffert) reported that Kevin G. Moore, a Logan County man, faces multiple charges, 
including conspiracy to impede officers, related to his actions during the January 6, 2021, Capitol breach. According 
to the article, the FBI's Pittsburgh and Washington Field Offices are investigating the case, which reveals that 
Moore and his co-defendant allegedly used Facebook Messenger to plan their involvement in the riot. The article 
noted that the U.S. Attorney's Office reported that over 1,504 individuals have been charged in connection with the 
Capitol breach, with ongoing investigations into additional suspects.
