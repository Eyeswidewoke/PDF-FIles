The Portland (ME) Press Herald (7/24, 244K) reported that according to state records in Maine, 
two former Franklin County Sheriff's Office employees are linked to Narrow Gauge Distributors, 
a marijuana business that the DEA and the FBI recently raided. The records show that "Brad 
Scovil of Farmington, a former lieutenant in the Franklin County Sheriff's Office, and Derrick 
Doucette of Livermore, the department's former canine officer, are the treasurer and a member 
of the board of directors of Narrow Gauge Distributors." An unidentified spokesperson "for the 
FBI's regional office outside Boston declined comment on what she called an ongoing criminal 
investigation." The Lewiston (ME) Sun Journal (7/24, 25K) also covered this story. 
Connection Between Search Operation In Maine, Drug Offender Highlighted. 
The Lewiston (ME) Sun Journal (7/24, Perry, 25K) reported that records show there is a 
connection between convicted drug trafficker Robert Sirois and a search operation that targeted 
two companies in Maine, The Home Grown Connection and Narrow Gauge Distributors. The 
Home Grown Connection, which the DEA and the FBI searched last week, is located in a 
building that Robert owns. That business, as well as Narrow Gauge Distributors, is owned by 
Robert's son, Maine resident Lucas Sirois. The Sun Journal added, "Federal authorities remained 
tight-lipped Thursday about the ongoing investigation involving marijuana-related businesses 
searched throughout" Maine, including the two Lucas Sirois-owned companies. The Sun Journal 
article is also posted to the Portland (ME) Press Herald (7/24, 244K) website. 
Authorities: 18 People Charged In Connection With Massachusetts Drug 
Investigation. 
The Worcester (MA) Telegram & Gazette (7/24, Petrishen, 124K) reported that federal 
authorities have announced that 18 people have been charged in connection with an 
investigation of an suspected illegal drug operation in Massachusetts. The article highlighted the 
FBI's involvement with the investigation. 
Suspect Arrested In Connection With Anti-Crime Operation Facing Drug Charges. 
The Kansas City (MO) Star (7/24, Stark, 549K) reported that Steven L. Younce has been 
charged in connection with a federal operation that aims to reduce violent crime in some US 
cities, including Kansas City, Missouri. The DEA was "involved in the investigation" that led to 
the arrest of Younce, who is "charged with possessing methamphetamine to distribute, 
possessing firearms in furtherance of a drug-trafficking crime and being a felon in possession of 
firearms." 
In an online story with similar coverage, KSHB-TV Kansas City, MO (7/24, 90K) reported 
that the federal operation which led to Younce's arrest includes agents from the DEA, the FBI 
and other federal law enforcement organizations. 
Federal Grand Jury Indicts Two Men Accused Of Distributing Fentanyl. 
The Sacramento M) Bee (7/24, Ahumada, 567K) reported that a federal grand jury recently 
indicted Steven Lawrence Robinson and Nathaniel Opondo Hubbert, who are "accused of 
distributing fentanyl, a dangerous synthetic opioid, that resulted in at least one overdose." 
Prosecutors "said Robinson and Hubbert conspired to distribute at least 40 grams of fentanyl 
from June 2 through June 24, according to the indictment." The article highlighted the DEA's 
involvement with the investigation of this case.
