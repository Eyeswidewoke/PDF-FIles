Issue Of West Bank Annexation Looms Over Israel-UAE Deal. 
Reuters (9/2, Al-Mughrabi, Williams) reports, "A difference between English and Arabic versions 
of a trilateral statement after an historic flight from Israel to the UAE has been seized upon by 
Palestinians to suggest the Gulf state has overstated Israeli readiness to drop West Bank 
annexation plans." The English version of the statement by the United Arab Emirates, Israel and 
the United States in Abu Dhabi "on Monday said the accord had 'led to the suspension of 
Israel's plans to extend its sovereignty". In contrast, "the Arabic version, carried by the UAE 
state news agency WAM, said 'the agreement ... has led to Israel's plans to annex Palestinian 
lands being stopped." Jamal Al-Musharakh, "chief of policy planning and international 
cooperation at the UAE foreign ministry, said the difference in wording was merely a translation 
issue." However, "Hanan Ashrawi, a senior PLO official, said it was a 'forked tongue' attempt to 
influence public opinion in the Arab world." 
Pompeo: Israel-UAE Deal Shows Trump's "Vision For Peace Beginning To Come 
To Fruition." Secretary of State Pompeo was asked on Fox News' Hannity (9/2, 535K) about

a nation has a right to exist. President Trump, his vision for peace is beginning to come to 
fruition." 
Saudis Allow Israeli Flights To Access Their Airspace For First Time. 
The New York Times (9/2, Specia, 18.61M) reports that on Wednesday, Saudi Arabia announced 
that it "would allow any flights going to and from the United Arab Emirates to fly over its 
territory, a move that would give Israel access to some of the kingdom's airspace for the first 
time." The Times says the Saudis' announcement, "made at the request of the United Arab 
Emirates, came days after the first direct flight from Israel to the emirates - a symbolic move 
as the two nations begin normalizing relations." Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu, "in 
a video statement delivered while standing in front of a map of the region, called the 
announcement a 'tremendous breakthrough:" 
The AP (9/2, Debre) reports that Saudi Foreign Minister Faisal bin Farhan al-Saud posted 
on Twitter "that the kingdom's 'firm and established position toward the Palestinian cause and 
people will not change." However, "he did not name Israel in his tweet."
