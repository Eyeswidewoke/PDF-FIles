Mexico Raises Spring Death Toll For 20 Of 32 States. 
Bloomberg (7/26, Zerpa, 4.73M) reports that Mexico "is raising its estimate for the death toll 
for about two-thirds of the country by 55% between mid-March and late June." Mexico "said in 
20 out of 32 states alone, fatalities from Covid-19 and other causes increased by 71,315 to a 
total of 202,077 from March 15 to June 27." The total "compares with the 130,763 earlier 
expected, according to a report by the health ministry on Saturday." Bloomberg adds that 
"43,680 have died in Mexico from coronavirus, the fourth-highest number of fatalities globally, 
according to Johns Hopkins University data." 
Spain Reimposes Lockdowns On Some Bars And Beaches. 
The AP (7/26, Wilson) reports that Spain's nightclubs, bars, and beaches "are facing new 
lockdown restrictions after turning into coronavirus hot spots. ... The northeast region of 
Catalonia now hosts two of the most worrying virus hotspots in Spain, prompting authorities to 
tighten restrictions in Barcelona and a rural area around Lleida that were relaxed only a month 
ago when Spain had its devastating outbreak in check." Meanwhile, Reuters (7/26, Melander, 
Allen) reports that the Spanish government "insisted" Sunday that the country is safe "after 
Britain abruptly imposed a two-week coronavirus quarantine on travelers returning from there, 
a decision that filled holidaymakers with dismay." Foreign Minister Arancha Gonzalez Laya told 
reporters, "Spain is safe, it is safe for Spaniards, it is safe for tourists." 
Russia Seizes Control Of Two Libyan Oil Facilities. 
The Wall Street Journal (7/26, Faucon, Malsin, Subscription Publication, 7.57M) reports Russian 
military contractors have seized control of two of Libya's largest oil facilities. In response, the 
US Treasury Department cited Russian involvement in Libya in a new round of sanctions applied 
in July to a Russian businessman with ties to President Vladimir Putin. The Department is also 
threatening sanctions against Russia's Libyan ally, Khalifa Haftar. 
Hook: Dispute Between Qatar, Neighbors Has Gone On Too Long. 
Reuters (7/26, Cornwell) reports US Special Envoy for Iran Brian Hook said Sunday that a rift 
between Qatar and its Gulf Arab allies "had lasted too long and urged them to rebuild trust and 
unity." During a visit to Qatar, Hook told reporters, "The dispute has continued for too long and 
it ultimately harms our shared regional interests in stability, prosperity and security. Bringing an 
end to this dispute really will advance the collective interests of all the parties to this conflict." 
McConnell, Rubio Defend NCSC Official Over Remarks On Election Interference. 
The Hill (7/26, Klar, 2.98M) reports Senate Majority Leader McConnell and Senate Intelligence 
Chairman Marco Rubio "defended a senior intelligence official over an assessment released 
Friday that warned of foreign election interference from Russia, Iran and China." McConnell and 
Rubio "also slammed Democrats" for their statement claiming William Evanina, the Director of 
the National Counterintelligence and Security Center (NCSC), of not going "far enough in 
alarming the American people" in the assessment. 
Debt Of Developing Nations Could Pose World's Next Economic Crisis. 
The Wall Street Journal (7/26, Chilkoti, Steinhauser, Subscription Publication, 7.57M) reports 
that the world is preparing for a battle over developing country debt as governments struggle 
to find money to prepare their health systems for C0VID-19 cases even as large amounts of 
their revenue are taken up by debt service.
